Ovella

A student-founded women's health technology initiative developing a wearable and companion app concept for endometriosis-related, menstrual, and pelvic pain support through warmth, gentle neuromodulation, tracking, personalization, and education.

The problem

Pain is too often normalized, minimized, or misunderstood.

1 in 10 women are estimated to be affected by endometriosis.
4-11 years is often cited as the range for delayed diagnosis.

Many of people deal with menstrual or pelvic pain without knowing how to explain it, track it, or ask for help. Endometriosis is still not widely understood, and it can be hard to tell when pain that feels "normal" every month is actually something that deserves more attention.

Ovella was created to help make that process less confusing. Through technology, education, and awareness, we hope to help people better understand their symptoms and feel more supported.

The solution concept

A wearable and app designed around comfort, awareness, and personalization.

01

Localized warmth

A noninvasive wearable pouch concept exploring controlled heat and temperature sensing to support comfort and help users understand repeated patterns.

02

TENS-style input

Gentle neuromodulation-style stimulation is being explored as an adjustable sensory support feature within safe, non-clinical prototype constraints.

03

Adaptive app

A companion app concept combines symptom check-ins, cycle context, session notes, and future personalization features based on user-entered experiences.
